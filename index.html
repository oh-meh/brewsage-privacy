Privacy Policy

Effective March 12, 2026

BrewSage is built for coffee lovers who want better results from their equipment. We believe your brewing data is personal, and we treat it that way. This policy explains what data BrewSage collects, how it's used, and what choices you have.

Data Stored on Your Device

The core of BrewSage is local-first. Your brew logs, bean library, machine settings, taste preferences, and ratings are stored directly on your device using a local database (SQLite). This data does not leave your phone unless you use features that require external processing, as described below.

On first launch, BrewSage generates a random device identifier (a UUID) and stores it locally on your device. This identifier is used for rate limiting and anonymous analytics. It is not linked to your name, email, or any personal information. You can reset it by reinstalling the app.

If you delete the app, your locally stored data is permanently removed.

AI-Powered Features

BrewSage uses the Claude API (provided by Anthropic) to power its AI features, including the Brew Coach, Sage recommendations, and bean label scanning. When you use these features:

Brew Coach & Sage: Your recent brew data (settings, ratings, flavor tags, bean and machine information) is sent to the Claude API to generate personalized coaching and bean recommendations. No personally identifiable information is included — only your brewing data.

Label Scanning: When you photograph a coffee bag label, the image is sent to the Claude API for text extraction. The image is processed and discarded — it is not stored by Anthropic. The extracted bean details are saved locally on your device.

API Proxy: In production, API requests are routed through a Cloudflare Worker proxy for security. Cloudflare may process request metadata (such as IP addresses and timestamps) per their privacy policy.

Anthropic's data usage policy applies to API interactions. Anthropic does not use API inputs or outputs to train its models.

Analytics & Crash Reporting

BrewSage uses a small number of privacy-focused services to understand how the app is used and to fix crashes. All of these operate in production builds only — nothing is tracked during development.

Anonymous Usage Analytics (PostHog): We use PostHog to understand how people use BrewSage — which features are popular, where people get stuck, and how to improve the app. Events tracked include: brew logged, bean added, brew coach tapped, and sage viewed. No personal information is attached to these events. Data is associated with an anonymous device identifier, not your name or email. See PostHog's privacy policy.

Anonymous Brew Telemetry (Supabase): If you opt in via Settings, BrewSage sends anonymous brew and bean data to help us understand brewing patterns and improve AI recommendations. This includes machine type, brew settings, ratings, and bean details (roaster, origin, roast level). No personal information is included. You can turn this on or off at any time in Settings. See Supabase's privacy policy.

Crash Reporting (Sentry): We use Sentry to detect and fix app crashes. When the app crashes, Sentry automatically collects diagnostic information including the error that occurred, device type, OS version, and app version. No personal information is collected — we have disabled collection of personally identifiable information. Crash reporting only runs in production. See Sentry's privacy policy.

Future Cloud Sync

We plan to introduce optional cloud sync in a future update, which would allow you to back up your data and access it across devices. If and when this feature is introduced, it will be opt-in, and this privacy policy will be updated to reflect the changes before launch. Cloud sync will never be required to use BrewSage.

Subscriptions

BrewSage offers an optional Pro subscription managed through Apple's App Store and Google Play. Subscription billing and payment processing are handled entirely by Apple and Google — BrewSage does not collect, process, or store any payment information.

We may use RevenueCat to manage subscription status. RevenueCat receives an anonymous app user ID to track your subscription entitlement — no personal information is shared.

What We Don't Collect

BrewSage does not collect your name, email address, location, contacts, or any personal identifiers. We use minimal, privacy-focused analytics to improve the app (described above). We do not use advertising SDKs, we do not sell or share your data with third parties for marketing, and we do not track you across other apps or websites.

Children's Privacy

BrewSage is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as BrewSage evolves — particularly when cloud sync or new features are introduced. Significant changes will be communicated through the app. The effective date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent version.

Questions?

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or how BrewSage handles your data, reach out at support@brewsage.ai.
